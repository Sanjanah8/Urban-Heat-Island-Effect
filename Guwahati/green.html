Urban Heat Island Mitigation

An urban heat island occurs when a city experiences much warmer temperatures than nearby rural areas.

URBAN HEAT ISLAND EFFECT

The urban heat island effect occurs when urban areas witness higher temperatures than their rural surroundings.

This is mostly due to human activities, buildings, and infrastructure in cities that absorb and retain heat more effectively than natural landscapes.

THE LEAST YOU CAN DO TO SAVE YOUR ENVIRONMENT

Plant more trees in your yard, community parks, and along streets.Trees provide shade, reduce surface temperatures, and improve air quality.

Opt for reflective roofing materials or cool roofs that reduce heat absorption. Reflective materials can help lower indoor temperatures and reduce energy consumption.

Install shade sails over windows and outdoor areas. Impact: These solutions reduce heat gain from sunlight and improve comfort.
